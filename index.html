You are part of something bigger.

Whether you are diving into the pool or pursuing other paths, your journey is unique. We invite you to join a groundbreaking study led by researchers from MIT, Harvard University, PSE, and UM6P to understand the real-world impact of coding schools on Moroccan Youth.

We are not just studying code; we are studying opportunity. By agreeing to participate, you help us understand how to build better educational systems for everyone in Morocco.

What does participation mean?

By clicking "I Agree," you join a select cohort of Moroccan youth whose career paths will inform future education policy.

This study involves research using an experimental admission procedure. As part of this research, admission decisions to 1337 are made using a randomized selection process rather than traditional admissions criteria. This experimental procedure allows us to rigorously evaluate the impact of the program.

Specifically, you agree to:

  • Share your application data: We will analyze the demographic and test data you have already provided to understand who is applying to 1337.
  • Let us follow your journey: We want to understand the full picture of your life and career over the next few years. We will contact you periodically to complete surveys about your employment, skills, earnings, and well-being.
  • Contribute to in-depth research: While some check-ins may be brief, other surveys may be longer and more detailed to help us deeply understand the challenges and successes you face in education and the labor market. Your thorough answers are vital to the study's success.

Expected Duration: This study will follow participants for approximately 3-5 years, with surveys conducted every 6 months. Each survey will take approximately 5-30 minutes to complete.

Your Safety and Privacy (MIT Standards)

  • Strictly Confidential: Your data will be "de-identified." We remove your name, phone number, and email before any analysis. Researchers at MIT or Harvard will see only numbers and trends, not "You."
  • Secure: All data is stored on encrypted, secure servers following strict US and International data protection laws.
  • Future Impact: De-identified data may be shared with the broader scientific community to help other researchers understand youth employment, but your identity will never be attached to it. If you do not want your de-identified data used for future research, please contact the research team.
